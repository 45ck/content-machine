A single notification can hijack your focus for twenty minutes. Your brain reads every buzz as a tiny maybe. Maybe it is urgent. Maybe it is social. Maybe it matters. That uncertainty is exactly what makes it sticky. You do not just lose the second you tap. You lose the restart after it. Researchers call that attention residue. Part of your mind keeps the last tab open. Apps exploit it with badges, streaks, and autoplay. So the best fix is friction, not motivation. Move the tempting apps, mute the cold alerts, and batch your checks. If your phone picks the timing, your brain never gets a clean run at one task.
