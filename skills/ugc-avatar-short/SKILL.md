# UGC Avatar Short

## Use When

- The short should look like a creator or spokesperson is addressing the
  viewer.
- The user wants product, testimonial, offer, or landing-page style
  content.
- Avatar or talking-head generation is acceptable as part of the visual
  plan.

## Core Approach

1. Write the presenter voice before choosing the avatar style.
2. Keep the presenter on the hook and the claim; use b-roll for proof,
   not filler.
3. Treat lip-sync or talking-head generation as one scene type, not the
   entire edit.
4. Use captions and overlays to sharpen claims, receipts, and CTA.
5. Reject uncanny or overlong presenter stretches; cut away sooner.

## Inputs

- offer, product, testimonial angle, or landing page
- optional avatar/photo/provider preference
- optional brand constraints and CTA

## Outputs

- presenter-oriented script
- avatar/talking-head scene plan
- supporting b-roll plan
- final short plus review bundle

## Optional Runtime Surface

- Use with [`faceless-mixed-short`](../faceless-mixed-short/SKILL.md)
  for mixed-source execution.
- Use [`video-render`](../video-render/SKILL.md) and
  [`publish-prep-review`](../publish-prep-review/SKILL.md) for final
  output and gating.

## Technical Notes

- Avatar scenes should carry claims, demos, or credibility.
- Do not keep the avatar full-screen for the entire short unless the
  performance is unusually strong.
- Product UGC still lives or dies on hook, proof, and pacing.

## Validation Checklist

- The presenter line sounds like a person, not landing-page copy.
- Cutaways and proof beats break up the avatar footage.
- Lip-sync or talking-head output looks good enough to survive mobile
  viewing.
- CTA is clear without turning the whole short into a hard sell.
- The result does not feel like interchangeable AI ad sludge.
